# Polaris Glossary

Version 1.0  
July 2026

---

## Purpose

Language shapes culture.

Polaris uses clear, consistent language so builders, engineers, and future stewards understand the system in the same way.

This glossary defines the official terms used throughout Polaris OS.

---

## Core Terms

### Builder

A person or organization creating, operating, or growing something of value.

A Builder may be an entrepreneur, owner-operator, manufacturer, farmer, contractor, professional, family business, or institution.

Polaris uses **Builder** instead of user or customer.

---

### Polaris OS

The operating system for builders.

Polaris OS connects business information, workflows, decisions, knowledge, and intelligence into one coordinated system.

---

### Polaris Labs

The organization responsible for designing, building, protecting, and improving Polaris OS.

---

### Command Center

A screen that helps a Builder understand and manage an important area of the business.

A Command Center must answer a meaningful business question.

Example:

Fleet Command answers:

> Which assets need my attention today?

---

### Memory

The trusted data layer of Polaris.

Memory stores company information, decisions, history, documents, relationships, and operational records.

Generic term:

Database

Polaris term:

Memory

---

### Engine

The business logic and coordination layer of Polaris.

The Engine processes rules, workflows, calculations, permissions, and decisions.

Generic term:

Backend

Polaris term:

Engine

---

### Studio

The interface through which Builders interact with Polaris.

Studio should always feel calm, clear, and simple.

Generic term:

Frontend

Polaris term:

Studio

---

### Gateway

A controlled connection between Polaris and another system.

Examples include QuickBooks, Motive, banks, email, calendars, fuel systems, and government portals.

Generic term:

API or integration

Polaris term:

Gateway

---

### Chief of Staff

The intelligence layer of Polaris.

The Chief of Staff observes, explains, prioritizes, recommends, and helps reduce Builder mental load.

It supports human judgment.

It does not replace human responsibility.

---

### Capability

A useful function that helps a Builder perform work or make a decision.

Generic term:

Feature

Polaris term:

Capability

---

### Command

A coordinated business area within Polaris OS.

Examples:

Fleet Command

Finance Command

Compliance Command

Dispatch Command

Builder Command

Generic term:

Module

Polaris term:

Command

---

### Builder Mode

A learning period in which Polaris observes real Builder work before proposing automation.

During Builder Mode, the goal is understanding, not coding.

---

### Engineering Mode

A building period in which observations are converted into designs, capabilities, tests, and releases.

---

### Builder Journal

The institutional record of observations, lessons, decisions, mistakes, milestones, and Builder experiences.

---

### Decision Record

A permanent explanation of an important product, architectural, cultural, or operational decision.

A Decision Record explains:

- what was decided,
- why it was decided,
- what alternatives existed,
- and what consequences follow.

---

### Constitution

The governing principles of Polaris Labs and Polaris OS.

The Constitution defines how Polaris behaves and what it must protect.

---

### Charter

The founding statement explaining why Polaris exists.

---

### Steward

A person responsible for protecting and improving Polaris.

A Steward does not merely contribute work.

A Steward leaves Polaris stronger than they found it.

---

### Builder Mental Load

The unnecessary burden created by unanswered questions, scattered information, hidden deadlines, unclear priorities, manual follow-ups, and constant context switching.

Reducing Builder mental load is a core responsibility of Polaris.

---

### Builder Clarity

The degree to which a Builder understands:

- what is happening,
- what matters,
- what requires action,
- what can wait,
- and what decision should come next.

---

### One Source of Truth

The principle that important business information should have one trusted home and should not be duplicated inconsistently across systems.

---

### Expedition

A meaningful release or milestone in the development of Polaris.

Generic term:

Release

Polaris term:

Expedition

---

## Language Rules

Polaris should prefer:

- Builder over user
- Command Center over dashboard
- Memory over database
- Engine over backend
- Studio over frontend
- Gateway over API
- Chief of Staff over AI assistant
- Capability over feature
- Command over module
- Steward over employee or contributor when referring to mission responsibility

Technical terms may still be used when precision requires them.

Polaris language should never create confusion merely for branding.

Clarity always comes first.
