# Decision Record DR-0002

## Title

Polaris Learns Before It Leads

---

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** July 2026

**Founder:** Surinder Pahil

---

## Decision

Polaris will spend dedicated time observing builders before attempting to automate their work.

Every major workflow should first be understood through real-world observation.

---

## Why

Software built from assumptions eventually becomes complicated.

Software built from observation becomes indispensable.

Polaris will be designed from real builder workflows, not imagined ones.

---

## Engineering Rule

Before building a major workflow:

1. Observe the builder.
2. Understand the workflow.
3. Identify friction.
4. Design the solution.
5. Build.
6. Observe again.
7. Improve.

---

## Weekly Rhythm

### Monday – Wednesday

Engineering Sprints

### Thursday

Builder Mode

Observe real work.

### Friday

Reflection

Improve Polaris based on observations.

---

## Builder Learning Principle

> Before Polaris automates work, Polaris must understand the work.

---

## Success Metric

The goal is not to add the most features.

The goal is to remove the most friction from a builder's day.

---

## Closing

Polaris is built **with builders**, not just **for builders**.

Observation is a first-class engineering activity and part of the culture of Polaris Labs.
