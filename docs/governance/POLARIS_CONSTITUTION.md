# Polaris Constitution

## Article I — Purpose
Polaris exists to help leaders make better decisions by transforming information into trusted, explainable intelligence.

## Article II — Human-centred intelligence
Athena augments human judgment. Recommendations must be explainable, traceable to evidence, honest about uncertainty, and open to challenge.

## Article III — Truth before speed
Fast answers are useful. Correct, transparent answers are more important. Weak evidence must be identified rather than hidden.

## Article IV — Architecture over convenience
Shortcuts must be intentional, documented, bounded, and revisited.

## Article V — Institutional memory
Polaris preserves decisions, rationale, commitments, outcomes, and lessons so organizational knowledge compounds.

## Article VI — Human control
Material actions remain under accountable human authority. Automation must expose what it will do, why, and with what evidence.

## Article VII — Continuous improvement
Each release must leave Polaris stronger through capability, reliability, usability, performance, security, maintainability, or documentation.
