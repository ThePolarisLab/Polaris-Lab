## Engineering Decisions

### PGE-004.3

- Refactoring recommendations are derived only from deterministic code-smell findings.
- Repeated findings may be grouped into one action when they share a target and recommendation.
- Priority is determined through explicit, validated severity weights and thresholds.
- Recommendations preserve source ranges, evidence, confidence, rationale, and guardrails.

### PGE-004.4

- Refactoring plans use deterministic impact estimates and stable dependency ordering.
- Impact values are directional planning estimates, not promises of exact post-refactor metrics.
- Missing dependencies and dependency cycles are treated as validation errors.
- Planning does not execute or rewrite source.
- The API exposes `GET /api/v1/refactoring/execution-plan`.
- ADR-013 records the architectural decision.

### PGE-007.2

- Evidence is assessed using explicit direction, relevance, confidence, and quality.
- Option scores preserve normalized breakdowns for evidence, benefits, drawbacks, risks, and constraints.
- Ties, missing evidence, critical risks, and mandatory constraints remain visible.

### PGE-007.3

- Recommendation outcomes are explicitly limited to `recommended`, `review_required`, or `insufficient_evidence`.
- Ties, weak margins, low scores, unresolved warnings, and missing evidence prevent fabricated certainty.
- Trade-offs are preserved dimension by dimension for every alternative.
- Confidence uses visible deterministic inputs and penalties.

### PGE-007.4

- Audit records preserve evidence traces, weighted contributions, alternatives, trade-offs, confidence, rationale, and warnings.
- Decision, evaluation, and recommendation artifacts must refer to the same decision.
- Unknown selected options are rejected.
- Replay fingerprints provide stable deterministic identity checks and are not cryptographic signatures.
- Audit generation does not approve, execute, or mutate decisions.
- ADR-014 records the architectural decision.

## Research Notes

- Refactoring recommendations become more useful when separated into detection, recommendation, impact estimation, and execution ordering.
- Dependency-aware planning requires stable topological ordering so identical inputs produce identical plans.
- Circular dependencies must be treated as invalid plans rather than resolved through hidden heuristics.
- Directional impact estimates are suitable for prioritization but should not be represented as guaranteed measured outcomes.
- Recommendation quality depends on preserving uncertainty, especially ties, narrow margins, low evidence coverage, and warning counts.
- Explainability requires a trace from final recommendation back to option scores, evidence assessments, and alternative trade-offs.
- Deterministic replay identifiers support audit comparison and debugging without pretending to be security signatures.
- A future orchestration layer should use these engines as governed tools with explicit inputs, outputs, and failure states.
